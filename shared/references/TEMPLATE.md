**Section A: Blog Post** (includes sections A1. Explanation and A2. Compelling Argument)

Write the blog post including the following aspects:

1. An introduction paragraph with a thesis and preview of the main points to come.
2. At least one paragraph explaining the health-related product, practice, or process. This satisfies section A1 of the rubric.
3. At least one paragraph providing a compelling argument discussing how the health-related product, practice, or process will benefit the employees or organization once it is implemented. This satisfies section A2 of the rubric.
4. In-text citations for at least three different sources. Each source should have corresponding reference list entries listed in section C: Sources. This also helps satisfy section A1 of the rubric.
5. A concluding paragraph that restates the thesis, reviews the main points, and presents closing comments.
6. The blog post should be a maximum of 1,000 words long.

**Section B: Identify Employee Group**

Provide a sentence or two that describes who the people are in this employee group being used as your audience for the blog post. This group should be employees within a healthcare organization, not patients or customers.

**B1. Analyzing Business Etiquette**

Explain how the audience described above influenced your choices in writing the blog post to keep with the expected etiquette for blog posts in the industry. Include what tone you chose and word choices you made while writing and why those were proper blog writing etiquette for this audience.

**Section C: Sources**

Provide the full reference list entry for your three outside sources used in the blog post. Include:

1. Author (or organization as author if no person)
2. Date (or n.d. if no date)
3. The title of the article, book, or website
4. The publication information (URL, publisher, or journal details)
